# Port of Entry

A free tool that helps online shoppers spot warning signs before they pay — and points them toward stores worth trusting.

**Live site:** _add your GitHub Pages link here once deployed_

## What it does

- **Link inspector** — paste any shopping link and get an instant checklist-based read on common scam patterns: missing HTTPS, brand lookalikes, link shorteners, suspicious domain endings, and more.
- **Store directory** — a curated, filterable list of legitimate online stores, including Zimbabwe-based retailers and major global marketplaces.
- **Field guide** — plain-language tips for reading a shopping page like an inspector would, covering the address bar, page content, and payment red flags.

## Why

Online shopping scams are common and hard to spot, especially for cross-border and diaspora-facing purchases. This tool doesn't replace judgment — it gives shoppers a fast, practical first check before they hand over payment details.

## Important note

The link inspector runs a local, rule-based checklist in your browser. It is **not** a live fraud database and cannot guarantee any site is safe — always verify independently before paying.
